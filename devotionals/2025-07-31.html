But to all who did receive him, who believed in his name, he gave the right to become children of God, who were born, not of blood nor of the will of the flesh nor of the will of man, but of God.
John 1:12-13 (ESV)

Born of God: The Divine Origin of Faith

Reflection:

What does this remarkable text teach us about becoming a child of God? John presents us with both a glorious promise and a profound mystery. To those who receive Christ and believe in His name, God grants the extraordinary privilege of becoming His children. But notice carefully—this is not merely adoption into a family; it is a fundamental transformation of our very nature.

The apostle emphasizes that this new birth comes “not of blood nor of the will of the flesh nor of the will of man, but of God.” Here we see the divine origin of saving faith laid bare. Our entrance into God’s family is not determined by human ancestry, personal desire, or another person’s decision. It flows entirely from God’s sovereign will and supernatural work.

This truth should humble us deeply. We cannot manufacture faith through religious heritage, personal effort, or human persuasion. The new birth is God’s work from beginning to end. Just as we had no control over our physical birth, we exercise no ultimate control over our spiritual birth. God alone breathes spiritual life into dead hearts.

Yet this same truth should also encourage us tremendously. If our salvation depended on our bloodline, our willpower, or human influence, how uncertain it would be! But because it rests on God’s unchanging character and sovereign purpose, it stands secure. The One who began this good work in us will surely complete it.

Consider the implications for daily living. If God has made you His child through divine birth, you possess a new nature that seeks to please Him. Your struggles with sin, while real, do not define your identity. Your failures, while grievous, cannot alter your standing as His beloved child. Your growth in holiness, while sometimes slow, is guaranteed by the same power that first gave you life.

This passage also shapes how we view others. We cannot argue, manipulate, or pressure anyone into the kingdom. Only God can open blind eyes and soften hard hearts. This frees us to share the gospel with confidence rather than anxiety, knowing that conversion belongs to the Lord.

Confessional Tie-in: “All those whom God hath predestinated unto life, and those only, he is pleased, in his appointed and accepted time, effectually to call, by his Word and Spirit, out of that state of sin and death, in which they are by nature, to grace and salvation, by Jesus Christ.” — Westminster Confession of Faith 10.1

Prayer Prompt: Father, thank You that my salvation rests not on my strength but on Your sovereign grace. Help me to live today as Your beloved child, trusting in Your power rather than my performance, and sharing the gospel with confidence in Your ability to save.

Go Deeper: Reflect on areas where you might be trusting in human effort rather than God’s grace for spiritual growth. Consider how understanding the divine origin of faith might change your approach to evangelism and your prayers for unbelieving loved ones.
